Traditional

Inspired by classic European decor, traditional design features rich colors, ornate details, and elegant furnishings. It often includes elements like wood paneling, elaborate moldings, and antique pieces.

Modern

Characterized by clean lines, minimalistic decor, and a neutral color palette with bold accent colors. It emphasizes simplicity and functionality.

Contemporary

Similar to modern design but more fluid and ever-changing, contemporary design incorporates current trends and often includes a mix of materials and textures.

Minimalist

Focuses on simplicity and functionality, with a clutter-free environment. It utilizes a monochromatic color scheme, open spaces, and essential furnishings to create a serene atmosphere.

Industrial

Inspired by warehouses and urban lofts, industrial design incorporates raw and unfinished elements like exposed brick, metal, and wood. It often features high ceilings and an open floor plan.

Scandinavian

Known for its simplicity, functionality, and minimalism, Scandinavian design uses light colors, natural materials, and clean lines to create a cozy and inviting space.

Bohemian

Eclectic and vibrant, bohemian design combines various patterns, textures, and colors. It often features vintage furniture, global-inspired accessories, and an overall relaxed, artistic vibe.

Farmhouse

A blend of rustic charm and modern comforts, farmhouse design uses reclaimed wood, neutral colors, and vintage accessories to create a warm and inviting space.
